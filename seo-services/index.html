SEO Services FAQs

Here are some of the most common questions clients ask about my freelance SEO services.

Which SEO service is right for my business?

That depends on where your website is right now and what you want to achieve. If you are unsure what is holding your site back, an SEO audit is usually the best starting point. If you already know you want ongoing support, I can create and manage a full SEO strategy for you.

For local businesses, local SEO may be the priority. For online stores, eCommerce SEO will usually be more important. I will always look at your website, your competition and your goals before recommending what you actually need.

Do you offer ongoing SEO support?

Yes. Ongoing SEO support is ideal if you want me to take responsibility for the strategy, implementation, monitoring and improvements over time.

This can include technical SEO, content recommendations, on page optimisation, internal linking, competitor research, reporting and prioritising the work that is most likely to move the needle for your business.

Can you just do a one off SEO audit?

Yes. A one off SEO audit is a good option if you want a clear picture of what is working, what is not working and what should be fixed first.

I can review the technical setup, content, keyword targeting, site structure, indexing, internal linking and wider organic opportunity, then give you practical recommendations you or your team can work through.

Do you work on local SEO and national SEO?

Yes. I work on both local SEO and national SEO. Local SEO is usually focused on visibility in specific towns, cities or regions, while national SEO targets broader search terms across the UK.

The right approach depends on how your business gets customers. Some businesses need a local strategy, some need a national strategy, and some need a mix of both.

Can you help with technical SEO fixes?

Yes. Technical SEO is a key part of the work I do. I can identify issues that affect crawling, indexing, site structure, page speed, redirects, canonicals, structured data and general search engine accessibility.

If development work is needed, I can provide clear recommendations for your developer or work directly with your team so the fixes are implemented properly.

Can you help with website migrations?

Yes. Website migrations need to be handled carefully because they can affect rankings, traffic and search visibility if redirects, URL changes and technical checks are missed.

I can help plan the migration, map redirects, check key pages, review the new site before launch and monitor the site after launch so any issues are picked up quickly.

Do you work with eCommerce and WordPress websites?

Yes. I work with eCommerce websites, WordPress websites and a range of other platforms. For eCommerce sites, the focus is often on product pages, category pages, technical structure and commercial search intent.

For WordPress websites, I can help with on page SEO, technical setup, metadata, content structure, internal linking and the plugins or settings that affect how the site performs in search.
